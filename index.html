Overpopulation:- A Growing Concern

Overview

Effect of Population Explosion on Environment

Human overpopulation stands as one of the most concerning environmental issues, slowly contributing to the forces behind environmental pollution, global warming and the consumption of natural resources at rates greater than that of their rates of regeneration. However, ecological issues are just the beginning.

As it stands, there are over 7.6 billion people in the world. It is true that there is overpopulation and that it is increasing. However, recent studies show that it is growing at a slower rate now than it was a few years before. These are positive signs, but the damage has been done and now methods of damage control have to be adopted. …

CASE I: Delhi

Delhi is the capital city of India and is seen by many as the heart of the nation. The city is known for its rich culture and tradition. Home to some famous historical structures, Delhi is still developing with the passing of time.

Delhi is the fifth most populous city in the world and the largest city in India area-wise. Delhi's 2021 population is now estimated at 31,181,376. Delhi has grown by 890,440 since 2015, which represents a 2.94% annual change. In Delhi, the pressure of such a rapid increase in population has already started showing. Be it water, power, education, health care, infrastructure, law and order or sanitation, all the systems are under immense pressure, which is only set to increase in the future.

Efforts Made by Delhi

A new law was also introduced which called for the mandatory planting of 10 saplings in case one tree is cut for construction purposes, which has helped increase the green cover in Delhi.

The Delhi government has introduced a new EV policy. The government will also provide subsidies and incentives on their purchase to promote large scale adoption of EVs

Anti-smog guns have been deployed at large construction sites amid rising pollution levels in the national capital. Over 10 anti-smog guns have been installed for dust control.
